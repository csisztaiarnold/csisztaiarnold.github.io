**[Against the Ice (2022)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt13873302/reference)** 🤷‍♂️  

Based on the true story about the 1909 Danish expedition in Greenland, starring Nikolaj Coster-Waldau, who you might know as the sister-shagger from Game of Thrones.  
Nothing outstanding about this movie, but it's good for a lazy afternoon passive watch while you occasionally browse stuff on your phone.

**[All My Friends Hate Me (2021)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt9340892/reference)** 🤷‍♂️ 

Why is this movie listed under the horror genre is above me. It looks and feels as a very typical modern british cringe-y comedy with a bit of a mistery twist. Not bad, but not too good either.  
I'd never recommend this movie to anyone, but if you run into it by any chance, I'd say go for it, it's worth a look.

**[Everything Everywhere All At Once (2022)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt6710474/reference)** 👍️

Ah, the chinese, overknotty ripoff of the Multiverse of Madness...

Well, it started off as really pretentious, exhausting and confusing, but after a while it slowly grew up on me. 
Loved that it didn't took itself too serious, I really dug the comedic elements of the movie.  
That said, I really really loathed almost all the characters for some reason.

Other than that, a quite solid take on the bolierplate multiverse plot.

Recommended, but I liked [Swiss Army Man](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt4034354/reference) much much better from the same director-duo.

**[Fresh (2022)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt13403046/reference)** 👍️️

Starts off as a comedy about modern dating, but suddenly turns into a rather amusing story. Avoid watching trailers or reading about the movie as it would ruin the surprise.

It's a directorial debut, which is a bit noticeable, but luckily they've got Pawel Pogorzelski ([Midsommar](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt8772262/reference), [Hereditary](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt7784604/reference), [Nobody](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt7888964/reference)...) as the director of photography, so the cinematography is absolutely fantastic.

**[King Richard (2021)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt9620288/reference)** 👍️️

A very very archetypical "based on true story" type of sport-movie, starring Will Smith as the father of Venus and Serena Williams.  
Actually I was more and more intrigued while they slowly introduced me to the girls' way to success, it was amusing and engaging.  
Everything seems very solid in this movie, especially Will Smith's acting.
