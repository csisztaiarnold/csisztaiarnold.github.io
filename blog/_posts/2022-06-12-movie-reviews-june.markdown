**[Against the Ice (2022)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt13873302/reference)** 🤷‍♂️  

Based on the true story about the 1909 Danish expedition in Greenland, starring Nikolaj Coster-Waldau, who you might know as the sister-shagger from Game of Thrones.

Nothing outstanding about this movie, and the CGI was outright awful, but it's good for a lazy afternoon passive watch while you occasionally browse stuff on your phone.

**[All My Friends Hate Me (2021)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt9340892/reference)** 🤷‍♂️ 

Why is this movie listed under the horror genre is above me. It looks and feels as a very typical modern british cringey comedy with a bit of a mystery twist. Not bad, but not too good either.

I'd never recommend this movie to anyone, but if you run into it by any chance, I'd say go for it, it's worth to take a look. 

<p align="center">...</p>

**[Everything Everywhere All At Once (2022)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt6710474/reference)** 👍️

Ah, the chinese, over-complicated ripoff of the Multiverse of Madness...

It started off as really pretentious, exhausting and confusing, but after a while it slowly grew up on me. 
Loved that it didn't took itself too serious, and I really dug the comedic elements of the movie.  
That said, I really really loathed almost all the characters for some reason.

Other than that, a quite solid take on the stereotypical multiverse plot.

Recommended, but I liked [Swiss Army Man](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt4034354/reference) much better from the same director-duo.

<p align="center">...</p>

**[Fresh (2022)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt13403046/reference)** 👍️️

Starts off as a comedy about modern dating, but suddenly turns into a rather amusing story. Avoid watching trailers or reading about the movie as it would ruin the surprise.

It's a directorial debut, which is a bit noticeable, but luckily they've got Pawel Pogorzelski ([Midsommar](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt8772262/reference), [Hereditary](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt7784604/reference), [Nobody](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt7888964/reference)...) as the director of photography, so at least the cinematography is excellent.

<p align="center">...</p>

**[King Richard (2021)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt9620288/reference)** 👍️️

A very very archetypical "based on true story" type of sport-movie, starring Will Smith as the father of Venus and Serena Williams.

Actually I was more and more intrigued while they slowly introduced me to the girls' way to success, the storytelling was engaging.

Everything seems quite solid in this one, especially Smith's acting.

<p align="center">...</p>

**[Ultrasound (2021)](https://imdb.com/title/tt12989912/reference)** 👍️️

Ultrasound is a sci-fi thriller, and a movie that definitely needs a second watch to get all the small details missed during the first viewing. 

It has a quite unique plot with a captivating flow, which is *really* confusing in the beginning, but starts to make sense as the movie progresses.

I really liked it, but it's definitely not for everyone. It's kind of a mix of Cronenberg and Lynch, so if you're a fan of at least one of them, check it out.

<p align="center">...</p>

**[Windfall (2022)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt15033192/reference)** 🤷‍♂️️

Ugh, all about this movie screams low budget.

After a guy breaks into a house of a Zuckerberg-type dude, he's surprised by the owners, and then a bunch of eye-rolling shenanigans happen. Yup, that's the whole story.

Maybe worth a watch, as the actors are okayish, especially Jesse Plemons, aka. Meth Damon.

The director has a much better movie I'd definitely recommend, [The One I Love](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt2756032/reference).

<p align="center">...</p>

**[The Innocents (2021)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt4028464/reference)** 👍️️

Advertised as a really disturbing movie about teens with weird superpowers, however, I wasn't really disturbed by anything in it (except maybe one scene). On the other hand, my girlfriend had to stop watching it after 30-40 minutes, so maybe I'm just desensitized to mildly unpleasant, creepy stuff 🤷‍♂

The atmosphere of the movie reminded me a bit of [Let the Right One In](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt1139797/reference), however, that movie does the grim mood much better.

<p align="center">...</p>

**[I Want You Back (2022)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt6462958/reference)** 👍️️

A likeable indie comedy about two dumped persons who find alliance in a hope to ruin their exes' new relationships.

Both my girlfriend and I liked the jokes and laughed out loud quite a few times. All the characters were charming and sweet, the actors made an excellent job of portraying them.

Maybe not the type of humor which is appealing for everyone, so can't recommend it wholeheartedly, but I'll definitely watch it again some time.

In case you liked this movie, I'd also recommend [Big Time Adolescence](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt3824648/reference) from the same director.

<p align="center">...</p>

**[Metal Lords (2022)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt6462958/reference)** 👍️️

Another indie comedy with an engaing story, this time about a metalhead and his friend forming a band and trying to compete on their school's Battle of the Bands.

What's interesting, that the quite humorous and delightful script was written by D.B. Weiss, one of the dingbats who ruined the last few seasons of Game of Thrones.

The young actors were absolutely fantastic (I have mainly remembered Jaeden Martell from [Knives Out](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt8946378/reference) where he was also remarkable).

<p align="center">...</p>

**[The Northman (2022)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt11138512/reference)** 🤯

My only high recommendation this time. I was anticipating it from the moment I've read that Eggers was directing a new movie, and just as every flick from him so far, it was absolutely fantastic, visually jaw-dropping.

Unfortunately, due to the huge studio budget, he was forced to release the "most entertaining" version of the movie instead of his own vision, and he said he would go back to smaller budget because the stress of making it work for the studio and a wider audience was too much.

Nevertheless, it was breathtaking, a movie I'll come back quite often.

<p align="center">...</p>

**[Freeheld (2015)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt1658801/reference)** 🤷‍♂

Another "based on a true story" movie with an LGBT theme that has a very recognizable low budget Hallmarkish production feeling.

It is about a lesbian police officer who's is trying to fight for her right to pass her pension to her partner after she's diagnosed with incurable cancer - so expect dramatic courtroom scenes and theatrical displays of emotions.

I kinda hated it and was rolling my eyes the whole time during the cliché-y scenes, but my girlfriend loved it, and the lead actors are actually fine, so it's maybe worth a check.

<p align="center">...</p>

**[White Boy Rick (2018)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt4537896/reference)** 🤷‍♂

Based on the happenings around the youngest (IIRC) FBI informant ever, which would be kind of an interesting story, but the movie wasn't too good with giving it back to me.

It jumps forward quite abruptly in timelines, the characters aren't really built up, and the movie ends all of a sudden.

The lead actor was appalling, who also took a lot out from the experience. 

Maaaaybe worth to watch once just to know about it.

<p align="center">...</p>

**[Minari (2020)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt10633456/reference)** 🤷‍♂

This movie was so generic I had to look up what was it actually about although I watched it just a couple of weeks ago and remembered the base story about a Korean family who's trying to build a farm somewhere in rural US.

It won an oscar for an Actress in a Supporting Role, and yup, that woman was the only part of the movie that stood out a bit.

**[Excision (2012)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt1984153/reference)** 🤷‍♂

An okayish horror with interesting visuals worth to look at only if you're a fan of the genre. Otherwise, avoid it.

<p align="center">...</p>

**[Toxikoma (2021)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt10735686/reference)** 🤷‍♂

Except some rare exceptions, I'm not a fan of modern Hungarian popular cinema at all, and in this regard, this one didn't disappointed me at all.

Based on a biographic novel about a self-absorbed drug addict actor who gets into a psychiatric hospital voluntarily to treat his dependence.

Mediocre story, mediocre direction, mediocre actors.

I was most suprised by the second-rate visuals, as the cinematographer, Péter Szatmári, was also responsible for the photography in [Womb](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt1216520/reference) and [Dealer](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0398013/reference), two of Benedek Fliegauf's most beautiful movies.

My girlfriend loved movie (and read the book too), but it's obvious that this movie is mainly directed to the more average movie-goer.

**[Black 47 (2018)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt3208026/reference)** 🤷‍♂

A drama about the Irish famine with godawful CGI which took me out from my viewing experience quite a few times.

Otherwise not a bad flick with good lead actors, definitely worths one watch.

<p align="center">...</p>

**[Men (2022)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt13841850/reference)** 👍

Another movie I was eagerly waiting for because I really admired the director's previous movies, but it wasn't really what I was expecting.

Similarly to [Annihilation](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt2798920/reference) from Garland, it deals with the trauma of loss, but unfortunately, does it in a much less graceful way. All the analogies seemed so ambitious and tiring for me, that I have started to zone out around the start of the third act - which was actually the best part of the movie, but I was already kinda over it, so for me, the end wasn't so distressing as it was probably intended to be.

I would recommend it nevertheless, as despite its weaknesses, it's still stands out from most the movies I've seen in June. The pacing is brilliant, and the cinematography and the sound design are remarkable.

<p align="center">...</p>

**[The Worst Person in the World (2021)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt10370710/reference)** 👍

Checked it out a few weeks after I've watched [Thelma](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt6304046/reference) from the same Norwegian director and expecting a similar atmosphere, but I couldn't be more wrong.

Based on the title and the trailer, I was assuming that I'll hate both the main character and the movie, but this is actually a laid-back comedy/drama about a young woman who struggles with her relationships and career. So really nothing too interesting about the story, but the acting and the direction was excellent.

Worth the watch.

#### Revisited

**[Doubt (2008)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt13841850/reference)** 👍

It's the third time I've seen this movie. It's based on a play, so the excellent performances were really needed to balance out the modest, limited sceneries.

It has an identifiable story which follows the clash of a nun and a priest based on the nun's suspicion that the priest is molesting children in their school. The ending is left open to interpretation of the viewer. I've had doubts of my own.

<p align="center">...</p>

**[Mary and Max (2008)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt0978762/reference)** 👍

One of my favourite (stop-motion) animated movies. I have seen it countless times, and now I wanted to rewatch it to show it to my girlfriend (she liked it but said that it was too sad).

It's about an outcast Australian girl who accidentally becomes a pen-pal with a man who lives in New York and suffers from Asperger's syndrome. Following their special, decades long friendship is an amusing rollercoaster of emotions.

The animation has a very distinctive, unique style and the humor is quite blackish sometimes, usually I just can't get off the smile of my face while watching this movie.
