<!--more-->

\
🤷‍♂️ - kinda okay (watch it and forget it)  
👍 - recommended  
🤯 - highly recommended  
🤢 - pure garbage

\
**[12 Strong (2018)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt1413492/reference)** 🤷‍♂️  

An okay war action movie based on true events, with Chris Hemsworth and Michael Shannon in leading roles. The director's (Nicolai Fuglsig) first movie, and it shows. Nothing outstanding here.

Watch it once and forget it.

<p align="center">...</p>

**[Adrift (2018)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt6306064/reference)** 🤷‍♂️  

Another "oh no, we're drifted to the open sea, what will we do now" survival movie, this time based on real life events and starring Shailene Woodley, who I really like, so the movie's creators could thank her for me managing to finish it all the way to the end. Otherwise, it's really nothing special.

Watch it once and forget it.

<p align="center">...</p>

**[At Eternity's Gate (2018)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt6938828/reference)** 🤷‍♂️

Willem Dafoe plays Vincent van Gogh in this one, and he's probably the only eminent part of the movie. I was expecting more vibrant color grading, considering the movie is about one of the most unique painters of our time - but apart from a few truly beautiful scenes, most of the movie looked dull and washed out.

Watch it once and forget it. If you want to see a better movie about van Gogh, check out Loving Vincent.

<p align="center">...</p>

**[Beautiful Boy (2018)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt1226837)** 👍

As far as drug related movies are considered, I found this one more disturbing than Requiem for a Dream. Amazing soundtrack, stunning performance from Timothée Chalamet, but I was slightly disappointed about Steve Carell.

Recommended.

<p align="center">...</p>

**[Behind the Curve (2018)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt8132700/reference)** 👍

A quite interesting, actually sympathetic documentary about The Flat Earth Society which addresses the general psychological aspects of people who believe and act on conspiracy theories. I enjoyed it thoroughly.

Recommended.

<p align="center">...</p>

**[Blaze (2018)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt6443294/reference)** 🤷‍♂️

What's with Ethan Hawke directing music dramas lately? Wasn't a too huge fan of the movie, but kinda OK I guess.

Watch it once and forget it.

<p align="center">...</p>

**[Boy Erased (2018)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt7008872/reference)** 👍

Joel Edgerton has proven again that besides his great acting roles, he's also a very talented director. The acting is exceptional, I'd specifically mention the astonishing performance of Lucas Hedges - hope he'll get more and more lead roles in the future.

Recommended.

<p align="center">...</p>

**[Can You Ever Forgive Me? (2018)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt4595882/reference)** 🤷‍♂️

A fairly forgettable movie with really annoying main characters and a quite stale story. That said, I really enjoyed Melissa McCarthy's and Richard E. Grant's acting.

Watch it once and forget it.

<p align="center">...</p>

**[Cosmopolis (2012)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt1480656/reference)** 🤢  

One of the worst Cronenberg movies I've ever seen in my life. A pile of pretencious, meaningless crap. Couldn't finish it.

<p align="center">...</p>

**[If Beale Street Could Talk (2018)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt7125860/reference)** 👍

I'm still perplexed why this movie wasn't nominated for Best Motion Picture, but hey, BlacKkKlansman, Black Panther or Bohemian Rhapsody must be much better than one of the best black people focused dramas from 2018. Anyhow, check this one out, it's really good, especially the acting.

Recommended.

<p align="center">...</p>

**[Everybody Knows (2018)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt4964788/reference)** 👍  

A quite good psychological thriller with Penélope Cruz and Javier Bardem. The director's (Asghar Farhadi) last few movies were amazing, and so was this one. Beautiful cinematography too!

Recommended.

<p align="center">...</p>

**[Green Book (2018)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt6966692/reference)** 👍

Green Book is a quite okay road movie, especially considering the director, Peter Farrelly, mainly made truly crappy movies till this one, so I was pleasantly surprised. Definitely didn't deserved the Oscar, though.

Recommended.

<p align="center">...</p>

**[Hearts Beat Loud (2018)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt7158430/reference)** 🤷‍♂️  

A fun and cute little indie movie starring Nick Offerman. It's not outstanding in any ways, but definitely worth to watch at least once if you're looking for a feelgood movie.

<p align="center">...</p>

**[Instant Family (2018)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt7401588/reference)** 🤷‍♂️

An endearing dramedy based on the director's own experience about adopting children. I wasn't expecting too much, but I actually laughed out loud on some parts. Can't say I'll keep this movie in my memories, but it's definitely worth to watch at least once.

<p align="center">...</p>

**[Last Flag Flying (2017)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt6018306/reference)** 👍  

I was postponing this one for a while, don't even know why. Really loved it, especially because of the strong casting. Steve Carell was incredible here!

Recommended.

<p align="center">...</p>

**[Lords of Chaos (2018)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt4669296/reference)** 👍  

As a former metal-head, I was definitely looking forward to a movie about the wake of Norwegian black metal and the leading figures around it, like Euronymous, Dead or Varg Vikernes. Well, I have definitely enjoyed it, although I wouldn't recommend it to everyone as it quite easily might leave sour taste in your mouth.

Recommended, depending on how interested you are in black metal.

<p align="center">...</p>

**[Mary Queen of Scots (2018)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt2328900/reference)** 🤷‍♂️  

Tedious and taxing. I realized pretty early in the movie that I'm not interested in the characters and the story at all, so the only thing that kept me watching the okay-ish cinematography and the costume design. And the casting was fine too, more or less.

Watch it once and forget it.

<p align="center">...</p>

**[Operation Finale (2018)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt5208252/reference)** 🤢

Uhh. Everything is really cringe-y about this movie. I've turned it off after 30 minutes.

<p align="center">...</p>

**[Overlord (2018)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt4530422/reference)** 🤷‍♂️  

Maybe I'm the only one, but I'm truly bored of the huge batch of nazi-zombie movies lately. This was eyerollingly cheesy and often boring, there's absolutely nothing noteworthy about it.

Probably worth to watch once if you're into this kind of stuff, though.

<p align="center">...</p>

**[Rebel in the Rye (2017)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt4986134/reference)** 🤢  

A biographical movie about J.D. Salinger. A really, really annoying character played by a mediocre actor. Also Kevin Rapey is in it, so I was vomiting a bit into my mouth a bit every time I've seen him on the screen.

Don't watch this crap.

<p align="center">...</p>

**[Rocco (2016)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt5788462/reference)** 👍  

A not too interesting documentary about Rocco Siffredi.

Eh, maybe it's worth to watch it once.

<p align="center">...</p>

**[Serenity (2019)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt6476140/reference)** 👍  

A hilariously awful movie! This one has a potential to become a "so bad that it actually good" masterpiece. Whatever the screenwriters were smoking during writing the script, I want some of that. I really can't imagine why would some A-List actors like Matthew McConaughey or Anne Hathaway think that it's a good idea to be in this movie after reading even three pages of the script (except the paycheck, that is). The direction was simply comical, but at least the terribleness kept me locked to the screen all the way to the end.

Definitely recommended for a good laugh and a couple of WTF moments.

<p align="center">...</p>

**[Spider-Man Into The Spider-Verse (2018)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt4633694/reference)** 🤯

One of the best high-budget animation features I've seen in the last few years, and definitely *the* best Spiderman movie I've ever seen. It's funny, smart, captivating and lovable. Everything is just so right about it!

Definitely recommended!

<p align="center">...</p>

**[Suspiria (2018)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt1034415/reference)** 👍  

A quite fine horror movie, one of the best ones I've seen from 2018 (besides A Quiet Place, Climax, Hereditary and The House That Jack Built).

Tilda Swinton was amazing as always, but I'll never understand why did they used her for a male role (and this isn't even a spoiler as it has nothing to do with the plot). It was really unnecessary and the uncanny "old man makeup on a woman" took away a lot from the scenes with the psychiatrist fellow.

Recommended.

<p align="center">...</p>

**[The Happytime Murders (2018)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt1308728/reference)** 🤢  

An action-comedy with dirty mouth muppets? Nah, I couldn't finish it.

<p align="center">...</p>

**[The Hate U Give (2018)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt5580266/reference)** 🤢  

Looks and feels like a melodramatic, clichéd Hallmark TV movie with really predictable writing, weak direction and sub-par acting.

I definitely wouldn't recommend it unless you have 133 minutes to spare on a second-rate black ghetto coming-of-age story.

<p align="center">...</p>

**[The Kindergarten Teacher (2018)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt6952960/reference)** 🤷‍♂️  

Nothing extraordinary here, but a quite okay drama.

Watch it once and forget it.

<p align="center">...</p>

**[The Miseducation of Cameron Post  (2018)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt6257174/reference)** 🤷‍♂️

This is the female version of Boy Erased. Not a too bad movie but I'll never ever get over Chloë Grace Moretz's distracting, awful acting in every single movie she's in.

Watch it once and forget it.

<p align="center">...</p>

**[The Mountain Between Us (2018)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt2226597/reference)** 🤷‍♂️  

 Another survival movie, this time in the cold mountains. The lead actors (Idris Elba and Kate Winslet) has less than zero chemistry, sometimes it feels like they were forced to work together. The highlight of the movie is a cute dog.

Watch it once if you're bored, you will probably forget it quickly.

<p align="center">...</p>

**[The Vanishing (2018)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt4131496/reference)** 🤷‍♂️  

Can't say too much about this one. Not bad, not too good either. The acting was good, haven't even realized for a while that the lead was actually Gerard Butler.

Watch it once and forget it.

<p align="center">...</p>

**[Thunder Road (2018)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt7738450/reference)** 🤯  

An absolutely fantastic drama about a police officer's meltdown and dealing with loss and grief after the death of his mother. The acting is incredible and memorable, especially considering that this is the leading actor's first full feature film.

Recommended!

<p align="center">...</p>

**[Vox Lux (2018)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt5960374/reference)** 🤢  

A total crapfest, do yourself a favour and just avoid this movie. Not even Natalie Portman and Jude Law could save it.
The only thing what had value in it was Sia's soundtrack.

<p align="center">...</p>

**[Widows (2018)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt4218572/reference)** 👍  

Steve McQueen makes some pretty fine movies, and this wasn't an exception. It was based on a TV series from the 80's and portrays a few women who are trapped into a situation which was escalated from the criminal activities of their husbands. Great acting, and as always, amazing cinematography from Sean Bobbitt.

Recommended.

### Series I've never seen before:

**[Kidding - Season 1](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt7375404/reference)** 🤯  


Wasn't was I was expecting, at all. Ended up binging the first season in a night. A very good, gritty, funny drama. One of Jim Carrey's best works.
Definitely recommended!

<p align="center">...</p>

**[Conversations with a Killer: The Ted Bundy Tapes](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt9425132/reference)** 👍   

A really well made documentary series about one of the most known American serial killers. I binged it in one night as I couldn't stop watching it.


Recommended!

<p align="center">...</p>

**[The Terror - Season 1](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt2708480/reference)**  👍  

Based on true events. This would be a truly amazing series if they'd omit the atrocious, horror-y polar bear subplot. But everything else in this series is absolutely gorgeous! Great acting, amazing cinematography. Despite some of its flaws, I'd definitely recommend it.

<p align="center">...</p>

**[Russian Doll - Season 1 (one and a half episode)](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt7520794/reference)** 🤢

A Groundhog Day ripoff with a really irritating, embarrassingly terrible lead character. The reason why I have lasted a bit more than one episode, is because they're really short.
